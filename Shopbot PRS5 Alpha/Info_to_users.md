# Info to users

Last update: 21/04/2026
by -ajk

## Purpose

It takes some effort to make something with a CNC for the first time. The aim of this document is to provide info and guidance to potential users.

## Operational safety

The Shopbot is one of the few machines in the Fablab that is used under Superuser supervision. There is currently no way of circumnavigating this. Copenhagen Fablab doesn't recognise external certifications.

As part of your first project you will get an introduction on how to operate the machine.

## How to get in

1. Make your design.

2D vector design (svg, pdf, dwg) or a 3D file (step, stp, Fusion360 project file)

There is no ultimate way of coming up with design files. It is very project and skill dependent. If this is your first time desinging something, the we suggest try to make a simple 2D project in Inkscape or equivalent.

2. Find a superuser who is willing to take you and your project on.

You can do this by reaching out on the Facebook group. Alternatively try your luck and talk to us in person during open hours.

3. Generate machine toolpath

For first timers and simple 2D cuts we suggest to bring your design with you and we'll help you to create a toolpath on the computer next to the Shopbot.

---

That's it. :) There is a small contribution of 100 kr. per job.

We are super keen on setting people up. However, please be patient as it requires a lot more time get started compared to conventional tools.

## For experienced users

Experienced designers have the option use other CAM tools. We have a tool library on our github page for Fusion360.

## Tools

If you need specific tools (unlikely if you are a beginner) it makes sense to buy cheap two so you can be sure that you can finish the job in case one of them breaks.
